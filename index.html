Places To Visit

Ranthambore National Park

Ranthambore National Park is a vast wildlife reserve near the town of Sawai Madhopur in Rajasthan, northern India. It is a former royal hunting ground and home to tigers, leopards and marsh crocodiles. Its landmarks include the imposing 10th-century Ranthambore Fort, on a hilltop, and the Ganesh Mandir temple. Also in the park, Padam Talao Lake is known for its abundance of water lilies.

Jogi Mahal

Jogi Mahal is a rich collection of the heritage of Jaipur Jogi Mahal in Ranthambore, which was initially built as a hunting lodge by the royal family of the Jaipur which was used by many Maharajas for the same purpose. Second largest Banyan tree of the India, standing next to the Mahal is worth an applause. The beautiful combination of the Padam Lake with the Mahal makes an adamant mark on the visitors memories. People here can make themselves comfortable and regain energy for the next trip. The availability of food and water at this attraction in Ranthambore makes this place more favorable for relaxing physically as well as mentally.

Wild Dragon Adventure Park

Wild Dragon Adventure Park is situated in Ranthambore, Sawai Madhopur (a city in Rajasthan). As evident from its name, the Wild Dragon Adventure Park is an amusement park that entertains adventure lovers, centered in an undisturbed, natural environment. It is engulfed by a scenic surrounding of gently sloping hills, and a crystal-clear lake that promotes photography, water-sports,, and trekking activities.
